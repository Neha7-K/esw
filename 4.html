Behavioral Analysis

Moisture Threshold:

Threshold : 17-17.7% The critical soil moisture level, where the water absorption capacity diminishes significantly, is observed around Day 4 and 7 when the soil moisture level is 17.2. We can observe that after certain amount of increase in soil moisture the temperature decrease is not that much.

Absorption Capacity:

On observing the above points lilly has a dynamic water absorption capacity, responding to changes in environmental conditions.But it is not as dynamic as Golden Eye-Grass. The absorption capacity of this plant is compartively high than Tumeric plant and lower than Golden Eye-Grass.We can observe changes in temperature with in a particular span of time.

Temperature Resilience and Adaptivity:

Lilly has a average temperature resilience, with the ability to regulate its temperature. Unlike from Golden Eye-Grass it is not that much able to stablise its temperature but it has some range which it so not cross for any amount of change in soil moisture.

Plant Response:

Leaf Temperature vs. Soil Moisture:

    Here Leaf temperature is inversly proportional to Soil Moisture on observing the ideal moisture levels are 14-16% because it is showing expected changes in temperature.(day 6 and day 7)
  • Up to Day 7: Leaf temperature shows a relatively stable trend with varying soil moisture levels.
  • Day 8: Average soil moisture on this day was the highest among all the other days but we cannot see much change in leaf temperature compared to the previous days. This indicates that the plant is able to regulate its temperature despite the high soil moisture.This can be assumed as the Threshold soil moisture of the plant.
  • Days 9 to 14 From day 9 we can observe the general trend again.
  • Day 14 The soil moisture level is 17.4(close to 18) and the leaf temperature is 27.2 which is almost similar to the temperatureof the previous day which indicates that the plant is struggling to absorb water and here we can confirm that the Threshold of the plant is around 18%.

    Leaf Temperature vs. pH :




    We can clearly see that the Leaf temperature of the this plant is inversly proportional to pH of the soil.Here the plant is able to grow well in the pH range of 5.5 to 5.8 which can be considered as the ideal pH range for this plant.But the general pH range of an average plant is 6-7 so we can say that this plant prefers a slightly acidic soil.
